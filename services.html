Services

Test. Repair. Rebuild.

From the half-inch irrigation backflow at your house to a twelve-inch reduced pressure assembly on a commercial fire system, we handle the test and the work that follows.

Core Services

Three Things, Done Right.

Backflow prevention is the only thing we focus on, and that focus is the reason we're good at it.

01

Annual Testing

Texas Plumbing Code requires backflow prevention assemblies installed against any health hazard to be tested annually. We perform the test, document the result, and submit the report to the appropriate water authority.

  • Residential irrigation and pool
  • Commercial restaurants and hotels
  • Industrial and medical buildings
  • Reports filed on your behalf
  • Failed-test diagnosis included
02

Repair

When a test fails, the assembly does not always need to be replaced. Most failures are caused by a worn check valve, a stuck relief, or debris in the seat. We diagnose, repair, and re-test on the same visit when parts are on hand.

  • Failed-test root-cause diagnosis
  • Component-level repair
  • Re-test and certify after repair
  • Honest assessment of replace vs. repair
03

Rebuild

Older assemblies, especially larger commercial sizes, often have years of service left if rebuilt properly. We strip the assembly, replace worn internals with OEM-spec parts, reseat the components, and certify the rebuild like a new install.

  • Full internal rebuild kits
  • Sizes from 1/2" to 12"
  • OEM-spec components
  • Certified after rebuild
Residential

Annual Test, In and Out.

Most residential tests take less than thirty minutes. We arrive in the agreed window, perform the test on the assembly serving your irrigation or pool line, document the result, and submit the report to your water authority. If you're unsure where the assembly is or whether you need a test, send us a photo and we'll tell you.

If the assembly fails, we walk you through the result, explain what's wrong, and quote the repair before doing any work. No surprise charges, no upsells.

Fire System Coordination

One Number. The Whole Job.

Backflow on a fire suppression system is only one part of a larger annual compliance picture. Riser inspections, pump flow tests, alarm inspections, and new fire system installations all come with their own certifications and reporting requirements.

We coordinate the work through trusted, vetted partners so you can book the entire job through one number. We handle the backflow side directly. The rest gets scheduled, tracked, and confirmed by us before anyone shows up at your property.
